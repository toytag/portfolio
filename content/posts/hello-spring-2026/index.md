Spring arrives late in New England. By the time the trees soften and the blooms finally show up, the light already feels earned: cool mornings, clear afternoons, and that distinctly northeastern feeling of color returning after taking its time.

Most of this set is digital: quick, bright, and forgiving, the kind of camera I can take on a walk without overthinking it. But I slipped one film frame into the gallery. I am not saying which one. If one photo feels a little slower, warmer, or just a bit different from the rest, maybe you found it.
