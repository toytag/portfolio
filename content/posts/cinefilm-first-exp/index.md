Shooting Kodak Vision3 5219 (500T) and 5207 (250D) in a Nikon FE and Pentax 17 offers a cinematic look, but not without trade-offs. <a href="#kodak-5219-shot-on-nikon-fe" style="color: rgb(0, 150, 200);">The 500T, tungsten-balanced for low light, tends to render scenes with a noticeable blue cast when shot in daylight without correction.</a> <a href="#kodak-5207-shot-on-pentax-17" style="color: rgb(200, 150, 0);">The 250D, daylight-balanced, often skews yellow in its default tones.</a> While both stocks feature advanced Dye Layering Technology that enhances dynamic range and detail in shadows and highlights, the color straight out of the scanner isnâ€™t always pleasing. Considerable amount of effort in post-processing or color grading is often needed to achieve a natural or consistent look. They remain interesting choices for photographers chasing a unique aesthetic, but they are far from plug-and-play.

Perhaps the <a href="#one-more-thing-kentmere-pan-200-shot-on-nikon-fe" class="dyn-bw">B&W</a> shots turned out better -- see for yourself at the end.
